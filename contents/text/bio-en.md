**Claudius Tiberiu Iacob is a Romanian composer working at the intersection of rigorous construction and symbolic weight.** His art music moves between sacred resonance and contemporary fracture — drawing on Byzantine modality, modal thinking, and liturgical shadow not as acts of stylistic piety, but as living structural pressures placed against dissonance, fragmentation, and abrupt changes of perspective. Transcendence, in his work, is rarely granted innocently; beauty, when it appears, is almost always shadowed by memory, fracture, or loss.

Formed across an unusually wide arc — Orthodox theology, classical studies, then composition at the [National Music University of Bucharest](https://www.unmb.ro/en) under figures such as Dan Dediu, Doina Rotaru, Ştefan Niculescu, and Liviu Dănceanu — he went on to complete a doctorate in musicology, _summa cum laude_, with a thesis on algorithmic aspects of musical composition, accompanied by original software of his own design. He is a member of the [Union of Composers and Musicologists](https://ucmr.org.ro/en/homepage-ucmr/) of Romania (**UCMR**).

Alongside a parallel career in software architecture, he has maintained a steady compositional output: on average two new works per year, in recent years predominantly for orchestra, each typically exceeding ten minutes. The catalogue spans chamber, choral, piano, orchestral, and electroacoustic writing, and includes works honoured in composition competitions both in Romania and abroad, as well as pieces acquired by the UCMR.

He has described his own music as _"unwonted and spontaneous, with peaks of humour, grotesque, reverie and meditation"_ — a characterisation that, for once, undersells nothing.
